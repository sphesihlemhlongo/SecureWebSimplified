Understanding HTTPS Certificates

What Are SSL/TLS Certificates?

SSL/TLS certificates are digital documents that authenticate a website's identity and enable encrypted communication between a user's browser and the server.

Key Components:

  • Issuer: The Certificate Authority (CA) that issued the certificate.
  • Validity: The period during which the certificate is valid.
  • Public Key: Used to encrypt data that can only be decrypted by the server's private key.

Why Are They Important?

Certificates ensure that your data cannot be intercepted or altered by attackers, protecting sensitive information like passwords and credit card details.
